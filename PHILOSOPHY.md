# CRAFT Philosophy

These are the foundational beliefs of CRAFT (Community for Reproducible AI Frameworks and Tools). They are stable and rarely change.

## 1. Learn it yourself or lose control

If researchers treat AI methods the way many have historically treated biostatistics — as something to delegate to a specialist and receive back as a finished product — they will create new bottlenecks and cede meaningful control of their own research pipelines. The researcher who cannot direct, audit, and modify their AI-assisted workflow is dependent on whoever can. These skills belong with the people asking the research questions.

## 2. Trust but verify

AI tools have improved substantially, but they remain poorly calibrated to their own limitations — they do not reliably know where their performance degrades. This means the researcher must be positioned to catch errors, and you cannot catch what you do not understand. A knowledgeable orchestrator is not optional — it is what separates rigorous use from reckless use.

## 3. Standardize or repeat the reproducibility crisis

Ad hoc AI workflows are no more reproducible than ad hoc manual methods — they are simply faster, which means the damage scales. If every researcher invents their own approach to AI-assisted extraction or synthesis, we produce more research that is just as fragile. Structured, auditable, and shareable workflows are not merely an efficiency question. They are a scientific integrity question.

## 4. Start by automating the boring stuff

Researchers who do not learn to use AI effectively will spend their finite time on work that is rapidly becoming automatable — manual literature screening, hand extraction of data from charts, routine statistical coding. Their peers who adopt these tools will do the same work in a fraction of the time and redirect their effort toward the parts of research that genuinely require human judgment: asking the right questions, interpreting findings in clinical context, and deciding what matters.
